The Lunar Landing

Featuring an Interview with Mr. & Mrs. Flint

Read Article

Intro

In July 1969, the Apollo 11 moon landing marked a defining moment in human history. To better understand its impact, I interviewed my grandparents, who were teenagers at the time, about their memories. The mission—featuring Neil Armstrong, Buzz Aldrin, and Michael Collins—was launched aboard the Saturn V rocket. The event had a profound and lasting impact on politics, culture, and society by introducing new ideas and excitement.

Interview

My grandfather recalled watching the moon landing on a black-and-white TV and later going outside to look at the moon, joking,

“Though I couldn’t see it from there.”

He noted that space knowledge was limited, with most people relying on media. News was brief, with just three channels—CBS, NBC, and ABC—saying the same things each night. Despite this, the country was fascinated. Businesses capitalized on the craze, marketing space-themed diners, candies, commercials, clothes, and even “astronaut foods.”

“A lot of the commercials on TV were based around space and what the astronauts would do, and you would love to have this stuff, and even new appliances they had.”

He felt the moon landing helped boost the economy and inspired technological advances, especially in computing. He emphasized that the technology used during the mission was greatly inferior compared to what we have today. The lunar landing and space race gave a need and reason for the further development of such technology; the progress of which is what we each carry in our pockets today.

My grandmother had a very different experience; she was in Switzerland in a small hotel midmorning. She recalled,

“We came downstairs… [to the] living room, and everyone was gathered around the little TV… in the living room. And we said, ‘What’s going on?’ And they said ’The Americans are landing on the moon.’”

The day after the landing, she recalled with amusement, her family was on a riverboat tour in Paris and they joked that “Papa was one of the astronauts.” Her experience highlights the moon landing’s international significance and global presence beyond the United States.

Conclusion

The moon landing was more than a physical achievement—it symbolized scientific and cultural progress. My grandparents’ memories offered a personal glimpse into the event’s importance and its lasting impact on their generation during the height of the space race.
