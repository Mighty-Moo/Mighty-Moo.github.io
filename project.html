The Lunar Landing

Featuring an Interview with Mr. Kevin F.

Read Article

Intro

In July 1969, the Apollo 11 moon landing became a historic event that has been cemented in memory. I interviewed my grandfather on this topic, who was an adolescent at the time of the event.

The moon landing consisted of three men: Neil Armstrong, Buzz Aldrin, and Michael Collins, who were delivered to the moon aboard the Saturn V rocket.

This event greatly affected the culture, society, and politics of the 20th century and the years that followed.

Interview

My grandfather shared that he was a young adult at the time and vividly remembered watching the event on his family’s black-and-white television set. Afterward, he went outside to gaze at the moon, humorously stating:

“Though [I] couldn’t see [the moon] from there.”

He reflected that most people back then knew very little about space and the moon. His knowledge, like many others’, came primarily from media coverage.

“We had three [news channels]… CBS, NBC, and ABC… and [the news stations] all said the same thing for 30 minutes a night.”

Despite the limited coverage, the nation was fascinated by space. And when many people like something, businesses often capitalize on it. He described how diners, candies, commercials, clothing, and even supposed “astronaut foods” flooded the market.

Advertisers began promoting unrelated products as if they were space-related. According to him, the moon landing likely helped boost the economy during that time. He also emphasized how the moon landing had a lasting impact on technology, especially with inventions like the computer.

Conclusion

The moon landing wasn’t just about reaching the moon; it was about proving that it could be done. It resulted in a powerful extension of science, culture, and technology.

My grandfather's memories helped me understand firsthand just how significant the moon landing was as a moment in our nation’s history. His perspective gave me a deeper understanding of what the space race meant to the people of his generation.
